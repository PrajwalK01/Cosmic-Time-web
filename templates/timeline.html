Timeline of Discovery

From Ancient Stargazers to Interstellar Voyagers

~1600 BCE

Nebra Sky Disc

The oldest known concrete depiction of the cosmos found in Germany, showing the Pleiades, Sun, and Moon.

~1000 BCE

Babylonian Astronomy

Early astronomers in Babylon begin systematic recording of celestial phenomena, identifying the periodicity of eclipses (Saros cycle).

~270 BCE

Aristarchus of Samos

Proposes the first known heliocentric model, suggesting the Earth revolves around the Sun, nearly 1800 years before Copernicus.

1543

Copernican Revolution

Nicolaus Copernicus publishes De revolutionibus orbium coelestium, placing the Sun at the center of the universe.

1609

Galileo & Kepler

Galileo turns the telescope to the sky, discovering Jupiter's moons. Kepler publishes his first two laws of planetary motion.

1687

Newton's Principia

Isaac Newton publishes Principia Mathematica, formulating the laws of motion and universal gravitation, uniting celestial and terrestrial physics.

1915

General Relativity

Albert Einstein publishes his theory of General Relativity, describing gravity not as a force, but as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass.

1929

The Expanding Universe

Edwin Hubble discovers that distant galaxies are moving away from us, implying the universe is expanding from a primordial "Big Bang."

1903

The Wright Brothers

Orville and Wilbur Wright achieve the first powered, controlled flight of an aircraft at Kitty Hawk.

1957

Sputnik 1

The Soviet Union launches the first artificial satellite, marking the beginning of the Space Age and the Space Race.

1961

Yuri Gagarin

Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human to journey into outer space, orbiting the Earth aboard Vostok 1.

1969

Apollo 11

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin become the first humans to walk on the Moon. "One small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

1977

Voyager Missions

Launch of Voyager 1 and 2. They carried the Golden Record and are now the farthest human-made objects, entering interstellar space.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope

Deployment of HST, revolutionizing astronomy with crystal-clear images of the deep universe, determining the age of the universe.

2015

SpaceX Landing

SpaceX successfully lands a Falcon 9 orbital class rocket booster, ushering in the era of reusable rockets and cheaper space access.

2021

James Webb Space Telescope

Launch of the JWST, viewing the universe in infrared to see the very first galaxies formed after the Big Bang.

2025+

Artemis & Mars

Humanity prepares to return to the Moon to stay (Artemis) and sets its sights on the first crewed mission to Mars.
