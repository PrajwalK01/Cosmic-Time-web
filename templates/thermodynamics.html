Why we remember the past but not the future

5. Entropy and Time

While the laws of physics are mostly time-symmetric (they work the same forward and backward), Entropy provides a direction. The Second Law of Thermodynamics states that the total entropy (disorder) of an isolated system can never decrease over time. This gives time its "arrow."

Formula:

ΔS ≥ 0

The Heat Death

If entropy continues to increase, the universe will eventually reach a state of maximum disorder and thermal equilibrium, known as the "Heat Death," where no more work can be extracted and time effectively ceases to have meaning.
