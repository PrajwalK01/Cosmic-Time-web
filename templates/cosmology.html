Cosmic Cycles

From the Yugas of India to the Cyclic Calendars of the Maya

1. Time Across Indian Cosmology

Indian cosmology describes time as relative and cyclic. A single day of Brahma (Kalpa) equals 4.32 billion human years. This represents extreme scaling of time across reference frames, intuitively mirroring the concept of gravitational time dilation where time flows differently depending on the gravitational potential (or "loka").

2. Linear vs Cyclic Time

Greek Chronos represents linear time (t), marching forward from a beginning to an end.
Mayan and Indian systems represent cyclic time, modeled mathematically as periodic functions like sine waves. In these models, "endings" are merely phase shifts into new cycles.
